Beam Search Algorithm


Overview

The goal of the optimal decoder is to find the most likely output symbol sequence for the input. However, there are an exponentially large number of such sequences and they cannot all be evaluated.

E.g. if you have a symbol set of 100 symbols, and produce a sequence of 30 symbols (e.g. a sentence with 30 characters in it), the number of possible sequences to evaluate before selecting the most likely one is 10030.

Beam search provides a practical solution to this by incrementally selecting only the K-most-likely symbols at each stage. So, that way, if you are outputting a sequence of 30 symbols, at each time, you retain the K most likely sequences so far, expand it out to K*V (V = vocab size, 100 in our example) and reduce it back to K by retaining the K most likely.

As a result, each extension by 1 symbil takes KV computations, and you can find a good guess for the most likely 30-symbol sequence in only 30KV computations, which is exponentially cheaper than full search.
